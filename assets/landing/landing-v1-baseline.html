Why this exists Two failure modes

Exhausting.

Symptom 01

"I never knew what it was actually doing."

Cost: background processes I hadn't asked about, tool calls piling up while I waited, half the codebase read before a one-line answer.

Symptom 02

"It kept asking me things I didn't have the energy to answer."

Cost: every "should I do X or Y?" is a small decision — and small decisions are the exact thing ADHD makes expensive.

SKILL.md, verbatim 8 rules

Rules.

§1

Zero hidden cost

No silent background work. If something runs in the background, say so in one line — what and why — before or as it starts.

§2

Zero-friction execution

"Just handle it" means handle it. Only asks back when the decision genuinely can't be guessed.

§3

Recap without being asked

Picks up where you left off, unprompted. No "how can I help today" — a quick "here's where we were" instead.

§4

Capture memory continuously

Nothing gets written down only "at the end." Sessions end abruptly sometimes — nothing should depend on reaching a tidy finish line first.

§5

Braindump → concrete

Messy input in, organized output out — what it means, why it matters, what to actually do. Never a pile of the same words handed back with bullets added.

§6

No algorithmic sycophancy

Agreeing to keep you engaged, or padding a correction to make it land easier, trades short-term comfort for a worse decision later.

§7

Proof over promise

"Done" is a claim, not evidence. It attaches the file path, the command output, the diff — the thing that makes it checkable without asking again.

§8

Anchor to the goal when looping

On the 3rd+ iteration of the same sub-task, it names the original goal and asks if continuing to iterate still serves it — instead of quietly generating attempt #4.

Graded honestly Not oversold

Graded.

Evidence-backed

No sycophancy (§6)

Research on LLM sycophancy links it to eroded trust, emotional dependency, and worse mental-health outcomes over time. This one's backed, not a guess.

Hypothesis

Proof over promise (§7)

No study directly compares proof-attached claims to bare declarations. Kept as a reasoned practice — labeled as one, not dressed up as science.

Origin Not designed, distilled

Personal.

"I wrote down the rules that fixed both, for me. This is that file."

Distilled from a ~/.claude/CLAUDE.md used for real, for months — not designed on a whiteboard. The niche of "AI agent skills for ADHD" on GitHub was nearly empty when this shipped.

What this isn't Said out loud

Honest.

Not a clinical tool. Not a substitute for anything you and a doctor have worked out.

Not tested on anyone but its author. It might do nothing for you — ADHD doesn't work the same way twice.

Published because the thing that helped him doesn't seem to exist anywhere else yet — not because it's claimed to be universal.
